# Week 1 Session 2: Build the master pin template in Canva

Time budget: 90 to 130 minutes of active work.

Scope of this session:

- Sign up for a free Canva account.
- Create a folder structure for the My Feng Shui Home pins.
- Build a reusable 1000x1500 master pin template.
- Lock the brand palette so it is one click to apply.
- Lay out the title block, accent line, and URL strip.
- Save the master and duplicate it as a chart variant frame.

Out of scope today (do not start these):

- Designing the actual compass chart pin (that is Session 3).
- Writing the palette spec file `spec/brand-pin-palette.md` (that is Session 2b, which Claude does after this session).
- Any additional pin layouts.
- Anything on the kua-calculator site or any payment work.
- Stripe, Lemon Squeezy, consultations.

Brand palette (locked, used every step):

- Paper background: `#fcfcf8`
- Deep ink (lines and text): `#0e3b2c`
- Accent clay (sparingly, never as a full background): `#d9531a`

## Part A. Sign up for Canva (free tier)

The free tier is sufficient for this entire session. Do not pay for Canva Pro.

A1. Open a browser tab and go to `https://www.canva.com/signup/`.

A2. On the signup page, choose one of the buttons: `Continue with Google`, `Continue with Facebook`, or `Continue with email`. The Google button is the fastest if a Gmail account is already in use.

A3. If `Continue with email` was chosen: type the email address in the field, then click `Continue`. Open the inbox in another tab, find the message from Canva, copy the verification code, paste it into the Canva field, then click `Continue`.

A4. When Canva asks `What will you be using Canva for?`, click `Personal`. This keeps the account on the free tier and skips team setup.

A5. When Canva offers a free trial of Canva Pro on the next screen, click the small `Skip` or `Maybe later` link in the corner. Do not click `Start free trial`.

A6. Wait until the Canva home page loads. The home page has a large search bar at the top and a left sidebar.

How to know it worked:

- The top right corner of the page shows a circle with the account initials or profile photo.
- The left sidebar shows `Home`, `Projects`, and other items.
- The page URL is `https://www.canva.com/` and not the signup page anymore.

Gotchas:

- Do not use a temporary or disposable email address. Canva will reject these.
- If Canva keeps pushing the Pro trial, look for the small text link that says `Skip` or `Continue with free`. Do not enter card details.
- If the verification code email does not arrive within two minutes, check the spam folder before requesting another code.

## Part B. Create the MFSH Pins folder structure

Folders live under `Projects` in the left sidebar. A free Canva account allows unlimited folders, with up to 200 items per folder.

B1. In the left sidebar, click `Projects`.

B2. Near the top right of the Projects area, click the button labelled `Add new`.

B3. In the small menu that appears, click `Folder`.

B4. In the folder name field, type `MFSH Pins` exactly, then click `Create`.

B5. Click the new `MFSH Pins` folder to open it.

B6. Click `Add new` again, then `Folder`. Name this subfolder `00 Masters` and click `Create`.

B7. Click `Add new` again, then `Folder`. Name this subfolder `01 Compass Chart` and click `Create`.

B8. Use the back arrow or the breadcrumb at the top to return to the `MFSH Pins` folder.

How to know it worked:

- Inside the `MFSH Pins` folder there are two subfolders visible: `00 Masters` and `01 Compass Chart`.
- Both subfolders are empty.

Gotchas:

- The `Add new` button is in two places on the page. Either one works. Use whichever is easier to see.
- Folder names are case sensitive on screen but not when searching. Keep the names exactly as written so future sessions match.
- Do not put any designs inside these folders yet. That happens in Part C and Part F.

## Part C. Create the master 1000x1500 design

This is the blank canvas the master template is built on. Pinterest pins use a 2:3 ratio, which is what 1000x1500 produces.

C1. Click `Home` in the left sidebar to return to the home page.

C2. Near the top right of the home page, click the button labelled `Custom size`.

C3. In the popup, confirm the unit dropdown shows `px`. If it shows `in` or `mm`, click the dropdown and change it to `px`.

C4. In the `Width` field, type `1000`.

C5. In the `Height` field, type `1500`.

C6. Click the button labelled `Create new design`.

C7. Wait for the editor to open. A blank white canvas appears in the centre. A top toolbar shows `File`, `Resize`, and other items. A left sidebar shows `Design`, `Elements`, `Text`, `Uploads`, and so on.

C8. At the very top of the editor, click the design title (it currently reads something like `Untitled design - Custom size`). The title becomes editable. Type `MFSH Master Pin 1000x1500` and press `Enter`.

C9. In the top toolbar, click `File`. Look for `Move to folder` in the menu and click it. If `Move to folder` does not appear under `File`, open the three-dot `More` menu near the top right of the editor and look there. In the popup, navigate to `MFSH Pins > 00 Masters` and click `Move here`.

How to know it worked:

- The canvas in the centre of the screen is taller than it is wide.
- The design title at the top reads `MFSH Master Pin 1000x1500`.
- The file is now in `MFSH Pins > 00 Masters` (verify by opening Projects in a second browser tab).

Gotchas:

- If the canvas looks square or landscape, the width and height were swapped. Close the design without saving and start again at C2.
- The unit must be `px`. Inches or millimetres at the same numbers would produce a huge file.
- Canva auto-saves every few seconds. There is no `Save` button. Look for the words `All changes saved` near the top right.

## Part D. Lock the brand palette so it is one click to apply

On the free tier, Canva does not give a saved Brand Kit palette, but it does keep a `Document colors` strip per design that holds every hex value already used in the file. The plan is to paint one tiny swatch in each brand colour so all three appear in `Document colors` and become one-click reusable for the rest of the session.

D1. In the left sidebar of the editor, click `Elements`.

D2. In the `Elements` search bar, type `square` and press `Enter`.

D3. Under the `Shapes` row, click the plain filled square. A square appears on the canvas.

D4. Click the square once to select it. A floating toolbar appears above it.

D5. In the floating toolbar, click the coloured tile (this is the fill colour control). A colour panel opens on the left.

D6. In the colour panel, click the row labelled `+ Add a new color` (sometimes shown as a plain `+` tile). A hex input field appears.

D7. Clear the field and type `fcfcf8`, then press `Enter`. The square turns ivory.

D8. With the square still selected, copy it: press `Ctrl + D` (Windows) or `Cmd + D` (Mac). A duplicate square appears next to it.

D9. Click the duplicate to select it. Click the fill tile, click `+ Add a new color`, type `0e3b2c`, then press `Enter`. The second square turns pool-table green (deep ink).

D10. Press `Ctrl + D` (or `Cmd + D`) again. Click the new duplicate. Click the fill tile, click `+ Add a new color`, type `d9531a`, then press `Enter`. The third square turns true orange (clay).

D11. Move all three swatch squares off the canvas, into the grey area to the right. They remain part of the file but will not appear in any export.

D12. Click any blank part of the canvas to deselect.

D13. Click the canvas background once. In the top toolbar, click the colour tile labelled `Background color`. The colour panel opens. Under the `Document colors` row, click the paper swatch (`fcfcf8`). The whole canvas turns paper.

How to know it worked:

- The canvas background is the warm off-white paper tone.
- Three small swatches sit just off the right edge of the canvas: paper, deep ink, clay.
- When the fill tile is clicked on any element, the `Document colors` row at the top of the colour panel shows all three brand hex values.

Gotchas:

- Hex codes do not need the `#` symbol in Canva. Type `fcfcf8`, not `#fcfcf8`.
- Do not delete the three off-canvas swatches at the end of the session. They are what keeps `Document colors` populated.
- Free Canva accounts do not have a global Brand Kit colour palette. `Document colors` only lives inside this one file, which is why the master file is the source for every future pin.
- Clay (`d9531a`) is the accent. It must never be the canvas background. Reserve it for one thin line or one small artwork detail per pin.

## Part E. Lay out the master template

The layout reserves the top third of the canvas for the title, the middle two thirds for variant artwork, and a thin strip at the bottom for the URL. A single clay accent line sits just under the title block.

E1. In the left sidebar, click `Elements`. In the search bar, type `line` and press `Enter`. Under the `Lines` row, click a plain straight line. A line appears on the canvas.

E2. Click the line to select it. In the floating toolbar, click the colour tile, then under `Document colors` click the clay swatch (`d9531a`).

E3. With the line still selected, drag the right end handle (the small dot at the right tip) inwards until the line is about 300 px wide. As the drag happens, a size readout near the line shows a width number; release when it reads about 300. If a numeric `W` (width) field is visible in the top toolbar, typing `300` into that field works too.

E4. Drag the line to roughly the horizontal centre of the canvas and place it about 500 px down from the top (a third of the way down). Canva shows pink alignment guides when the line is centred.

E5. In the left sidebar, click `Text`. Click the button labelled `Add a heading`. A heading text box appears on the canvas.

E6. Click into the heading and type a placeholder: `Title Goes Here`. This is only a placeholder and will be replaced per pin.

E7. With the text box selected, open the font dropdown in the top toolbar. Type `Cormorant Garamond` in the font search. From the results, click `Cormorant Garamond`. The text changes to a refined serif.

E8. Set the font size to about `72` in the size box of the top toolbar. Adjust later if the title text feels too tight or too loose.

E9. Click the colour tile in the top toolbar, then under `Document colors` click the deep ink swatch (`0e3b2c`).

E10. Click the alignment button in the top toolbar until the text is set to centre aligned.

E11. Drag the heading so it sits roughly centred horizontally and about 300 px from the top of the canvas. Canva pink alignment guides confirm centred placement.

E12. Click the clay accent line. Drag it so it sits about 60 px below the bottom of the title text. Make sure it is centred horizontally.

E13. Click `Text` in the left sidebar again. Click `Add a subheading`. A new text box appears.

E14. Click into this text box and type `myfengshuihome.com` exactly. Do not include `https://` or `www.`.

E15. In the top toolbar, set the font for this text box to `Cormorant Garamond` as well, so the URL strip matches the title.

E16. Set the font size to about `28`.

E17. For widely spaced small caps: look in the text toolbar for an icon labelled `Spacing` (often two horizontal arrows pointing apart). Click it. A panel appears. Drag the `Letter spacing` slider until the value reads about `200`. If `Spacing` is not visible in the main text toolbar, open the three-dot `More` menu in the text toolbar and look for `Spacing` there. For uppercase: either retype the text directly as `MYFENGSHUIHOME.COM`, or look for a `Letter case` or `Aa` toggle in the text toolbar (or under `More`) and switch it to uppercase.

E18. Click the text colour tile, then under `Document colors` click the deep ink swatch (`0e3b2c`).

E19. Drag the URL text to the bottom of the canvas. It should sit centred horizontally and about 50 px above the bottom edge of the canvas. Canva pink alignment guides confirm centred placement and the bottom margin.

E20. Click anywhere blank to deselect. Step back from the screen for a moment and look at the whole canvas. The title sits in the top third, a thin clay line sits just below it, the middle two thirds is empty paper, and a small dark URL line sits at the bottom.

How to know it worked:

- The canvas reads: title at top, thin clay accent line under it, empty middle, URL strip at bottom.
- All text is deep ink. The only clay element is the thin accent line under the title.
- The middle two thirds of the canvas is empty paper.
- The status near the top right says `All changes saved`.

Gotchas:

- Do not fill the middle area with anything. That space is reserved for the chart artwork in Session 3.
- Clay (`d9531a`) appears once, as the small line under the title. Do not paint backgrounds, headings, or the URL strip in clay.
- If the title font does not show as Cormorant Garamond after selecting it, check the font search spelling. The free tier has six Cormorant variants. Pick `Cormorant Garamond` specifically (not `Cormorant SC`).
- Pixel positions above are guides, not exact. Use the alignment lines Canva shows while dragging. Centred and balanced matters more than exact px coordinates.
- Part E is the time sink in this session (often 30 to 45 min). Plan for it.

## Part F. Save the master and duplicate it as the chart variant frame

Canva auto-saves. There is no save button. The duplication creates a second file in the chart subfolder, leaving the master untouched as the source.

F1. In the top toolbar of the editor, look for the words `All changes saved` near the right side. If those words are visible, the master is saved.

F2. In the top toolbar, click `File`, then click `Make a copy`. A duplicate design opens. In most cases it opens in a new browser tab; in some Canva builds it opens in the same tab. Either way, the design title at the top will change to `Copy of MFSH Master Pin 1000x1500`.

F3. In the duplicate, click the design title at the top of the editor. Replace the title with `MFSH Variant Compass Chart 1000x1500` and press `Enter`.

F4. In the duplicate, click `File`, then `Move to folder`. If `Move to folder` does not appear under `File`, open the three-dot `More` menu near the top right of the editor and look there. Navigate to `MFSH Pins > 01 Compass Chart` and click `Move here`.

F5. In the duplicate canvas, click the placeholder title text. Edit it to read `Compass Chart` instead of `Title Goes Here`. Leave the font, size, colour, and position alone.

F6. Do not place any chart artwork yet. The middle two thirds stays empty paper for now. The chart goes in during Session 3.

F7. Wait until the duplicate shows `All changes saved` at the top.

F8. If the duplicate opened in a new tab, close it and switch back to the master tab. If it opened in the same tab, open `Projects > MFSH Pins > 00 Masters` and click the master thumbnail to reopen it.

F9. In the original master tab (or reopened master), confirm the design title still reads `MFSH Master Pin 1000x1500` and the placeholder title still reads `Title Goes Here`. The master was not edited.

How to know it worked:

- Two designs exist: `MFSH Master Pin 1000x1500` in `00 Masters`, and `MFSH Variant Compass Chart 1000x1500` in `01 Compass Chart`.
- Both have the same layout: title at top, clay accent line, empty middle, URL strip at bottom.
- The variant title text reads `Compass Chart`. The master title text still reads `Title Goes Here`.

Gotchas:

- Do not edit the master after duplicating. The master is the source for every future variant. All edits happen in the variant copy.
- `Make a copy` is the correct command. Do not use `Duplicate page` from the page menu, because that creates a second page inside the same file rather than a separate file.
- If the duplicate ends up in the wrong folder, open it, click `File > Move to folder` (or `More > Move to folder`), and move it. The folder location is part of the deliverable.

## Quick reference

- Master file path: `Projects > MFSH Pins > 00 Masters > MFSH Master Pin 1000x1500`
- Variant file path: `Projects > MFSH Pins > 01 Compass Chart > MFSH Variant Compass Chart 1000x1500`
- Title font: Cormorant Garamond, deep ink (`#0e3b2c`)
- URL font: Cormorant Garamond, uppercase, wide letter spacing (~200), deep ink
- Background: paper (`#fcfcf8`)
- Accent: clay (`#d9531a`), used as a single short horizontal line under the title only

Recommended serif font: Cormorant Garamond. Its refined low-contrast strokes read as calm and literary at large pin sizes while staying legible on small Pinterest thumbnails, which fits the calm-practitioner voice better than the slightly more decorative DM Serif Display.
