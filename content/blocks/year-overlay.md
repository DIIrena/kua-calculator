# Your 2026, in outline

Everything else in this report is fixed. Your Kua number, your group, your four good directions and your four cautious ones - those belong to you and do not change from one year to the next. This chapter is about a second layer that sits on top of all of that and moves on its own schedule.

## The annual layer

The tradition reads each solar year as having its own arrangement. A few corners of any space are treated with extra care that year, handled gently, kept calm rather than disturbed. A few other corners are read as supportive, the ones the year leans toward. The important thing to understand is that this set shifts. The corner the tradition handles carefully in one year is not the same corner it handles carefully in the next. This is a rotating layer, separate from the steady personal directions that make up the rest of your reading.

So you are always holding two maps at once. The first is yours and permanent: the directions tied to your Kua. The second is the year's, and it turns. Neither cancels the other. The skill is in reading them together.

## How to think about the overlap

You do not need the full annual chart to use this idea. You only need to notice where the two maps meet.

- **Where the year's cautious corner lands on one of your own cautious directions** - your {{huoHaiDir}}, {{wuGuiDir}}, {{liuShaDir}}, or {{jueMingDir}} - the tradition reads it as a quiet corner for that year. The simple move is to let it stay quiet. Not the place for the year's disruption, renovation, or the start of something demanding.
- **Where the year's supportive corner meets one of your good directions** - your Sheng Qi ({{shengQiDir}}) or your Tian Yi ({{tianYiDir}}) - the tradition reads it as a corner worth using that year. The kind of spot to favour when you have a choice about where to sit, work, or place something that matters to you.

Most years, the maps will agree in some corners and pull against each other in others. That is normal. As a reader in the {{kuaGroup}} group, your fixed directions are the constant you measure each year against. You are not chasing the year; you are checking where it reinforces what you already know about your space and where it asks for a lighter touch.
