# Two people, one home

A home is rarely arranged for one body. When two people share it, the eight directions get read twice, once for each of you, and the two readings do not always agree. This page is about how the {{kuaGroup}} group meets the other group under one roof, and how couples settle the places where the two maps point different ways.

## The two halves of the compass

Every Kua number sorts into one of two groups, and the groups split the compass cleanly in half. You are in the {{kuaGroup}} group, Kua {{kuaNumber}}, so your four supportive directions sit on one side and your four cautious ones on the other.

A partner in the **same group** shares that same half. Their Sheng Qi, Tian Yi, Yan Nian, and Fu Wei land in the same four directions as yours, even if the order within those four differs. A partner in the **other group** holds the mirror image: their four good directions are your four cautious ones, and your four good directions are their cautious set. Neither map is wrong. They are simply reversed.

## When you share the group

If both of you sit in the {{kuaGroup}} group, the shared furniture is easy to agree. The bed can take a direction that is favourable for both. A pair of dining seats can both face good directions at once. The desk in a shared study can point somewhere the tradition reads kindly for either of you. You are choosing among four directions you both like, so the only question is which of the four, not whether anyone loses out.

Your relationship direction, {{yanNianDir}} (Yan Nian), is the natural default for the shared bed here, since the tradition gives it to connection and steady partnership.

## When you are in different groups

If your partner is in the other group, the shared pieces need a rule, because what helps one of you is the cautious side for the other. Couples handle this with a few plain compromises:

- **The shared bed by season.** The headboard takes one partner's good wall for a stretch, then can shift to the other's when the season changes. A push season favours one head; a recovery season can favour the other's {{tianYiDir}} (Tian Yi) wall.
- **Split the difference on solo pieces.** The desk and a personal reading chair are single-user, so each of you turns your own chair to your own favourable direction. No compromise needed; you are not sharing the seat.
- **The relationship direction as the tie-break.** For the one bed you both sleep in, {{yanNianDir}} is the traditional shared-furniture choice, because the tradition treats it as the connection direction rather than one partner's win.

## Same group vs different group

| | Same group as you | Other group |
|---|---|---|
| Their four good directions | Same as yours | Mirror of yours |
| Shared bed | Any direction you both like | Alternate by season, or use {{yanNianDir}} |
| Shared dining seats | Both face good directions | One compromises, or both take {{yanNianDir}} |
| Solo desk / chair | Each faces own direction | Each faces own direction |

## Three refinements for couples

**The bedroom's own wealth pocket.** The bedroom carries a miniature map of its own, and practitioner material sometimes activates its far-southeast pocket as a couple's shared-building corner: one small paired token there, and nothing more. No fountain, no water feature; water stays out of bedrooms. The relationship logic of the southwest remains the primary read; this pocket is for couples who want the room to hold both stories, rest and shared plans, without importing office energy.

**The formal pair.** Mandarin ducks are the familiar couple symbol; the dragon and phoenix together are the formal one, the imperial couple, yang and yin in balance. Their place is the southwest relationships sector or the couple's living space, and the rule that matters is always as a pair; a lone phoenix carries widowhood symbolism in some traditions and is avoided in relationship placements.

**Earth, not fire.** Western styling codes romance as fire: flames, red, candlelight. The Chinese relationship sector runs on the opposite logic; the southwest is earth, the element of stability and endurance, and its activators are grounded, paired, and calm. By all means keep the candles for the evening itself. For the standing arrangement of the relationship corner, think stone and pairs rather than heat.
