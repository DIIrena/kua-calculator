# Your Yan Nian direction is {{direction}}

Yan Nian is your *relationship* direction, the one the tradition assigns to the parts of life that need to **last**. For you, {{firstName}}, it points **{{direction}}**.

To know what that quality is meant to feel like, picture a room doing the work well. It is nearly midnight and two people who love each other are still at the kitchen island. The dishes are stacked. Two coffee cups sit between them, one long cold. Somebody's phone buzzed twenty minutes ago and neither of them got up. They are not solving anything. They are inside the quiet between two people who do not need to fill the silence, and the room is letting them stay there.

## What this means

The full name is *Yan Nian* (<span lang="zh-Hant">延年</span>), literally **"longevity"** - lengthening the years. If Sheng Qi is for action and Tian Yi is for recovery, Yan Nian is for *connection*: the marriage, the friendship that survives three moves and one bad year, the sibling called when nothing in particular has happened. The name does not promise a long life or a lasting partnership. It names the kind of arrangement the tradition reads as holding across long time, and it gives that quality a wall in your home.

Hold the claim loosely, because it is small and testable. Some configurations of body and direction are *said to* make the conversations that build connection feel slightly easier. You cannot prove a seat lengthened a friendship. You can notice, over a season, whether a corner became the one your household keeps returning to.

In the star vocabulary this direction is **Wu Qu, the Military star**. The name sounds severe until you remember what long campaigns actually require: loyalty, endurance, and people who hold the line beside you for years. That is why the tradition hands Yan Nian the marriage bed, the dining table, and every seat where a bond matters more than a victory.

## Do this

- **Point a couple's shared headboard at the {{direction}} wall**, so both heads rest toward it. Where Tian Yi is the healing headboard, Yan Nian is the relationship one: the orientation the tradition ties to a partnership ageing well, rather than to waking sharp.
- **Take the {{direction}}-facing chair for shared decisions.** Most households have one seat where the bills get opened and the Christmas plans get argued. Sit there with your body facing {{direction}}.
- **Angle the shared corner toward it.** The sofa you both land on after dinner, the kitchen island where the long talks happen: arrange the seating so both bodies open loosely toward {{direction}}.
- **Solo readers, claim it for the long call.** The tradition's "relationship" is wider than a marriage: friendship, parents, children, the people kept across decades. Your Yan Nian corner is the chair the fortnightly phone call lands in, the window seat a long letter gets written from.

## Avoid this

- **Do not file this chapter under couples-only.** That is the most common way readers waste their relationship direction. Yan Nian is for *unspectacular* shared time, repeated: the Tuesday dinner, the standing call.
- **Do not fight over whose direction wins in a shared bed.** Partners often have different charts. There is a fair way to settle it, and it has its own section in the hard-cases chapter near the end of this book.
- **Do not give Yan Nian the ambitious work.** It is the wrong job. The push seat belongs to Sheng Qi; this direction keeps the conversations, not the deadlines.

## Practitioner tip

The bed question I am asked most by couples is Tian Yi or Yan Nian, and my answer is a question back: what does the bed mainly need to be right now? If one of you is running on empty and sleep is the scarce thing, the healing headboard earns the wall. If the sleep is fine and the two of you keep missing each other in the week, give the wall to Yan Nian for a season. Both are correct readings with different jobs, and a headboard can change jobs when the season changes.

## A real home

Ines lives alone. She is Kua 1, so her Yan Nian points south. Her flat's south side held nothing but a narrow window ledge and a chair that had drifted there when the movers left. She noticed, filling in her Room Map, that this accidental chair was already where she took the Sunday call with her mother and the occasional two-hour call with her oldest friend. So she committed to it: the chair got the good cushion, a small table for the tea, a lamp that did not glare, and its seat turned so her body faced the south window. Nothing about her life reorganised itself. But the calls got longer and the chair stopped being accidental, and when a friend stayed over that autumn, it was the corner they both gravitated to without being told. The tradition would say the Yan Nian corner was finally being used for its job.

## Activating your corner, the traditional way

Beyond furniture, the older books give each Kua a small recipe for *activating* the Yan Nian corner - the folk layer of the tradition, offered here in the same honest frame as everything else. For your Kua, the recipe is to {{yanNianActivation}}.

Hold this as custom, not mechanism. A wind chime does not negotiate with anyone, and a pair of ducks has never saved a marriage. What the recipe does is what all good customs do: it keeps one corner of your home deliberately tended, in your own chart's colours, as a standing reminder of what you want that corner to hold. If the objects appeal to you, place them and enjoy them. If they do not, a clean, well-lit, cared-for {{direction}} corner carries the same intent with no props at all. Whatever you place, tend it; the tradition is unanimous that a neglected activator reads worse than an empty corner.

## If the chair is still empty

You may be reading this single, with one place setting that gets used. The tradition's version of this is gentler than the magazine version. It does not claim that pointing your bed to Yan Nian delivers a partner by spring. It says the room *prepared* for the kind of relationship you want, before it arrives, is the room it reads as more ready to support one: the headboard on your {{direction}} wall, the second chair already at the small table rather than stored against a wall. The body learns what a room is for. What the practice offers, stripped of metaphysical confidence, is a room that is ready. The arrival is its own thing.
