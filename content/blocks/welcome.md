# Welcome, {{firstName}}

Pick an ordinary morning. The kettle is beginning to sound, light has reached one familiar surface, and for no dramatic reason the room feels easy to be in.

Then think of another morning in the same home, when the chair, the bed, or the angle of your body seemed slightly wrong. Nothing terrible happened. The room simply felt less supportive than it might have.

Feng shui begins with noticing differences like these. The Eight Mansions tradition offers one structured way to explore them, using your Kua number and the eight compass directions. It is not a prediction about your life, and it cannot tell you exactly how a room will feel. What it gives you is a map of traditional placements to try, observe, and adapt in your own home.

Your calculated Kua number is **{{kuaNumber}}**, which places you in the **{{kuaGroup}} group**. The pages ahead explain the four directions the tradition considers supportive for you, the four it generally treats with more caution, and a few simple experiments you can try without redecorating.

A note on what this guide is, and is not. It is personalised to your **direction profile** - the eight directions that follow from your Kua number. It does not know the layout of your home, the rooms you have, or where your windows fall. So you will not find instructions like "move the sofa." You will find traditional placements and a clear way to test them against the home you actually live in. You stay the expert on your own rooms. The tradition just hands you the compass.

Read it once for the shape of it. Read it again with a pencil, marking the one or two moves you could actually make this week. That is all it asks.
