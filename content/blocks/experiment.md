# Your seven-day compass experiment

You do not need to rearrange your home to use this book. You need to change one thing, leave it for a week, and pay a little attention. Here is the method, followed by the log page that keeps you honest.

## Step 1: Pick one move

Choose a single adjustment from your supportive chapters, in rough order of how easy each is to try:

- **The desk.** Turn your chair so your body faces a supportive direction while you work. Easiest, because the chair rotates freely.
- **The reading chair.** Angle the chair you actually rest in so your body opens toward a supportive direction.
- **The dining seat.** Take the seat that lets you face a supportive direction during shared meals.
- **The bed.** Move the headboard to a supportive wall, so the top of your head points that way. Highest effort; attempt it only if the furniture moves easily.

Pick one. Just one. Changing everything at once tells you nothing about what, if anything, made the difference.

## Step 2: Write your before-note

Before you move anything, write two sentences on the log page: which direction the chair, seat, or bed currently faces or points, and how that activity has felt lately. Not a diagnosis, just a plain note. "I read in this chair most evenings and tend to drift off." "Dinners feel rushed." This is your before, it takes thirty seconds, and it is the only way to know whether anything changed.

## Step 3: Make the move and leave it alone

Adjust the one thing. Then leave it for seven days and use the space the way you normally would. Resist the urge to keep fiddling; the point is a clean test, not a renovation. One line per evening on the log is plenty: a word or two about how the seat, the meal, or the waking felt.

## Step 4: Read your log and decide

At the end of the week, read the before-note and the seven lines, and ask one honest question: does anything feel different? **If it feels better, keep it.** You have found a placement chosen by a structured method instead of guesswork. **If nothing changed, you have lost nothing but minutes.** Move it back, or try a different supportive direction, or a different piece of furniture. We will not tell you what you are supposed to feel. The tradition offers the placement; your own week is the test.
