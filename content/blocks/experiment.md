# Your seven-day compass experiment

You do not need to rearrange your home to use this guide. You need to change one thing, leave it for a week, and pay a little attention. Here is a simple way to do that.

## Step 1: Pick one move

Choose a single adjustment from the four supportive chapters. The four most common starting points, in rough order of how easy they are to try:

- **The desk.** Turn your chair so your body faces one of your supportive directions while you work. This is the easiest move, because the chair usually rotates freely.
- **The reading chair.** Angle the chair you actually sit in to read or rest so your body opens toward a supportive direction.
- **The dining seat.** At the table, take the seat that lets you face one of your supportive directions during shared meals.
- **The bed.** If the room allows it, position the bed so the headboard sits against one of your supportive walls, so the top of your head points that way as you sleep. This is the highest-effort move; only attempt it if the furniture moves easily.

Pick one. Just one. Trying to change everything at once tells you nothing about what, if anything, made a difference.

## Step 2: Note where you started

Before you move anything, write down two things in a sentence each:

- Which direction the chair, seat, or bed currently faces or points.
- How that activity has felt lately. Not a diagnosis - just a plain note. "I read in this chair most evenings and tend to drift off." "Dinners feel rushed." "I wake up groggy more often than not."

This is your before. It takes thirty seconds and it is the only way to know whether anything changed.

## Step 3: Make the move and leave it for seven days

Adjust the one thing. Then leave it alone for a week. Resist the urge to keep fiddling; the point is a clean test, not a renovation. Use the space the way you normally would.

## Step 4: Notice, without forcing it

At the end of the week, read your before-note and ask one honest question: does anything feel different? Maybe the chair is easier to settle into. Maybe nothing changed. Both are useful answers.

- **If it feels better,** keep it. You have found one placement that works for you, chosen with a structured method rather than guesswork.
- **If nothing changed,** you have lost nothing but a few minutes. Move it back, or try a different supportive direction, or try a different piece of furniture.

We will not tell you what you are supposed to feel. The tradition offers the placement; your own week is the test. That is the whole method, and it is one you can repeat for any room, any season, for as long as you live in the home.
