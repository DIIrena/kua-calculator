## What the Kua number is

Your Kua is a single digit between one and nine, with no five, calculated from your birth year and your gender. It is not your astrology. It is not a fortune. The most useful way to hold it is as a **coordinator**: a non-arbitrary way to choose between options that otherwise look equivalent.

There are eight compass directions a person can face. Most of the time you do not notice which one you are facing, because most of the time it does not seem to matter. The Eight Mansions tradition proposes that, for any given person, four of those eight directions are treated as supportive and four are treated with more caution. Your Kua number is what sorts them.

The system has been the standard structured framework in Classical practice for around four centuries, resting on a yin-yang and trigram cosmology older still. It survived because households found it gave them something concrete to decide with: where to put a bed, which seat the head of the family takes at dinner, which way the cook faces at the stove. That is what it is for. It does not predict your future. It gives you a structured way to choose.

## What East group means

The eight Kua numbers split into two groups. East group is 1, 3, 4, and 9. West group is 2, 6, 7, and 8. Yours is East. The split is the most consequential division in the system: a direction the tradition reads as supportive for an East group person is one it reads as cautious for a West group person.

The four directions the tradition considers supportive for East group are:

- **North**, the direction of water.
- **South**, the direction of fire.
- **East**, the direction of wood and morning sun.
- **Southeast**, the direction of wood and abundance, traditionally called the wealth direction.

The four it treats with more caution are northeast, southwest, west, and northwest.

Look at the supportive four. Three of them - water, wood, wood - are elements the tradition associates with *motion*. Water flows, wood grows. South is the direction of being seen. The tradition reads East group as oriented toward expansion: learning, building, generating, being recognised. That does not mean every East group person is loud. Plenty are quiet. It is a description of the qualities the tradition associates with your favourable directions, not a personality test.

## The Kua and the elements

A short detour, because it makes the rest of the guide easier to read. The five elements of Chinese metaphysics - wood, fire, earth, metal, water - each have a direction, a colour family, and a season.

- **Water** is your north: cool, deep, the colour of a dark river. Associated with career and learning.
- **Wood** is your east and southeast: green and brown, the colours of growing things. Spring.
- **Fire** is your south: bright red, the direction of recognition. Summer.

The two elements not on your supportive list, earth and metal, sit in your cautious directions. This does not mean you should strip earth tones and metallics from your home. It means you now have a vocabulary for what each colour and direction is doing, so you can choose rather than guess. The later chapters give you the specific tools.

## Sharing a home with someone of a different group

About half of all couples are mixed: one East, one West. In that case your supportive directions are largely your partner's cautious directions, and the reverse.

The classical answer is not to optimise the whole home for one of you. It is to assign each *function* to the person whose use of it matters most. The shared bed orients to whichever partner's sleep is hardest to protect. The shared desk, if there is one, to whoever does the more demanding work there. The dining table seats each of you in your own supportive direction. Most homes have more rooms and corners than they have people, so this is usually less of a compromise than it sounds. The deeper version of this analysis is its own product, *The Couple Compatibility Compass*. For now, it is enough to know the two halves of the compass are not the same, and the home is large enough for both.

## The four qualities, named

Each of your four supportive directions carries a traditional name. You will read each one its own short chapter, so here they are only in brief:

- **Sheng Qi** - the generating direction, the strongest of the four. For active, ambitious functions.
- **Tian Yi** - the health direction. The most common choice for the bed in ordinary seasons.
- **Yan Nian** - the relationship direction. For shared furniture and connection.
- **Fu Wei** - the anchor. The mildest. For study, stillness, and steady focus.

Your four cautious directions are Huo Hai, Wu Gui, Liu Sha, and Jue Ming. We spend less time on them, and we treat them as lower-priority placements rather than as anything to fear. You will read each in its own short chapter too.
