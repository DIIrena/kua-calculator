# Welcome, {{firstName}}

Pick an ordinary evening at home. You move through the rooms without thinking about them, and most of the time they hold you well enough that you never stop to ask why. Now and then a corner feels off, a seat feels wrong, and you shift without naming the reason. This report gives you a way to name it.

The Eight Mansions tradition works from your Kua number and the eight compass directions. Your calculated Kua number is **{{kuaNumber}}**, which places you in the **{{kuaGroup}} group**. From that one number the tradition derives eight personal directions: four it reads as supportive for you, and four it treats with more caution. That full reading is the spine of what follows.

## What is in this report

This is the extended reading, so it goes past the eight directions into the rooms where you actually spend your hours.

- **Your eight directions in full** - the four the tradition gives to support, from {{shengQiDir}} through {{tianYiDir}}, {{yanNianDir}}, and {{fuWeiDir}}, and the four it reads with care, {{huoHaiDir}}, {{wuGuiDir}}, {{liuShaDir}}, and {{jueMingDir}}.
- **Pairing with another person** - how the {{kuaGroup}} group sits beside someone whose number falls in the same group or the other one.
- **The bedroom**, the desk, and the dining table - one section each, on how the tradition orients the body in the place built for rest, the place built for work, and the place built for sharing a meal.
- **The 2026 solar year**, read against your own directions rather than as a general forecast.

<p class="pull-quote">This is a map of placements to test in your own home, not a prediction about your life.</p>

The report does not know your floor plan, your windows, or which rooms you have. So you will not find an instruction to move the sofa. You will find traditional placements and a clear way to test each one against the home you live in. You stay the expert on your rooms. The tradition just hands you the compass.

Read it once for the shape of it. Then read it again with a pencil, marking the one or two moves you could actually make this week.
