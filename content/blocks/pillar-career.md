# Your career corner, {{firstName}}

The career life-area is fixed in the **North**. On the map of your home, North is North for everyone, so this corner sits in the same place for you as it does for anyone else. What changes from person to person is not where the corner is, but how your own directions land on top of it. We will get to that overlay at the end. First, what the tradition puts here.

## What the North holds

The tradition reads the North as the sector of **path, work, and forward motion**, the long line of a working life rather than any single job. Where some sectors are about people or rest or recognition, this one is about movement: the direction you are heading, the way the next stretch of road opens up in front of you. The classical labels are career, life path, and new beginnings. Hold all three loosely. The point is not a promotion. The point is *flow forward*, the sense that the path is open and that you can move along it.

Because the theme is movement, the tradition is most concerned with whether this corner is **clear and moving** or **stuck and stagnant**. A North corner buried under stored boxes, a North wall that nothing can pass, a North room that has become the place where unfinished things pile up: each of these is read as a path that has silted up. The reading does not promise that clearing the corner advances your career. It treats a clear, moving North as the configuration that matches an open path, and a clogged North as the one that matches a path you cannot see down.

## Water is its element

The element the tradition gives the North is **Water**, and Water is the key to everything else in this corner. Water is the element of flow, of finding the low route, of moving around obstacles rather than stopping at them. It is also the element of depth and of quiet, a river that is moving even when its surface looks still.

This is why the tradition treats *moving* water as supportive of the North and *stagnant* water as working against it. The image to hold is a stream, not a puddle. A few ways the tradition keeps Water in this corner:

- **Actual moving water**, kept gentle and clean. A small tabletop fountain that flows *inward* toward the room rather than out toward a door or window. The inward-flow rule matters: water flowing out of the space reads as the path draining away. Stagnant or stale water works against the cure, so it has to be kept fresh.
- **The colours of Water** (deep blue, black, charcoal) as accents rather than a whole wall.
- **Undulating, flowing shapes** rather than hard blocks. Wavy lines, soft curves, gentle movement.
- **Metal**, lightly, because in the productive cycle Metal feeds Water. Round metal shapes, white and metallic tones, a single clean metal object support the Water that holds the corner.

What the tradition keeps *out* of the North follows the same logic. A great deal of Earth crowded into this corner is read as damming the Water, since Earth controls Water in the elemental cycle, so a heavy, cluttered, stuck North is doubly off-theme. And the most-flagged problem of all is simple stagnation: clutter, broken things, and anything that has stopped moving.

<p class="pull-quote">Water finds the low road and keeps going. The tradition asks only that you keep the channel open.</p>

## Keep it clear and moving

If you do one thing with the North corner, the tradition is clear about which thing: **keep it clear and keep it moving.** This is the practical heart of the sector and it costs nothing.

- **Clear the obvious blockage first.** If the North corner is where storage has accumulated, that is the single move. A path that is physically blocked is the literal version of the thing the corner is about.
- **Repair anything broken there.** A door that sticks, a drawer that jams, a lamp that does not light. Broken things in the corner of forward motion read as friction on the path, and clearing them removes a small background load whether or not you hold the symbolic claim.
- **Bring light into it.** This corner should not be the darkest one in the room. A path you cannot see down is harder to walk than one that is lit.
- **Introduce one moving element** and keep it moving. A fountain you actually maintain, or, if a fountain is impractical, a single image of clear, moving water in the same spot. A piece of art that reads as an open road or a flowing river carries the same theme.

The maintenance is the cure. A fountain left to go stale, or a "clear" corner that quietly fills again over a month, gives the corner the stagnant reading rather than the moving one. The tradition weights the *ongoing* state of the corner, not the afternoon you tidied it.

## The desk's link to this

The North is the home of career, and the object most tied to career is the **desk**. So the desk is where this corner becomes a decision you can actually make, because a desk is almost always rearrangeable when a North wall is not.

Two things the tradition asks of a desk, in order:

1. **Command position first.** The desk should have a solid wall behind it so your back is supported, and a view of the door so you see who enters without turning, while not sitting directly in the line of the door. This is the single most-cited working-seat rule and it comes before any direction work. If your back is to a window or an open doorway, a high-backed chair plus something solid behind you partially restores the backing.
2. **Then the direction your body faces.** What the tradition reads at a desk is where the *body* looks: the chair you turn, not the way the desk happens to point. For ambitious, forward-pushing work, the tradition turns the body toward your **generating direction, {{shengQiDir}}**. That is the orientation it associates with drive and active work.

You do not need the desk to physically stand in the North corner of the room to work with the career theme. The corner is the room's career sector; the desk is your personal career instrument. Treat them as two separate levers. Keep the North corner clear and moving as the room-level move, and set the desk in command position facing {{shengQiDir}} as the personal move. For most people whose work is the thing they are pushing forward, the desk is the lever that matters most, because it is the one they control every day.

## Your Kua overlay

Here is where your own numbers meet this fixed corner. You are Kua **{{kuaNumber}}**, in the **{{kuaGroup}} group**, and the question is how your eight personal directions sit on top of the North.

**Check your North.** North is a fixed sector, so it may land on a supportive direction for you or on a cautious one. This reading cannot assume which, and you should not either. So check it against your own list:

- If **North is one of your four supportive directions** ({{shengQiDir}}, {{tianYiDir}}, {{yanNianDir}}, or {{fuWeiDir}}), then this corner is doubly strong for you. The fixed career sector and one of your personal good directions sit in the same place, and the tradition reads that overlap as a reinforcing one. Lean into it: this is a natural spot to keep clear, light, and moving.
- If **North is one of your four cautious directions** ({{huoHaiDir}}, {{wuGuiDir}}, {{liuShaDir}}, or {{jueMingDir}}), treat the corner gently. The advice does not change much: keep it clear, keep it moving, do not pile it high. The tradition simply asks you not to make it the corner where you sit for hours or sleep with your head; handle it lightly and let the desk carry the personal work instead.

**Check {{shengQiDir}} for the desk.** Your generating direction is {{shengQiDir}}, and that is the one the tradition pairs with the desk for active work. This is a separate check from the North corner: the corner is fixed in the North, but the *direction you face at the desk* is yours to choose, and {{shengQiDir}} is the supportive one to aim the chair toward. If the room only lets you face a different wall, face any of your other three supportive directions ({{tianYiDir}}, {{yanNianDir}}, or {{fuWeiDir}}) before you settle for a cautious one. The rough adjustment, any good direction over any cautious one, is the one the tradition weights most. The fine adjustment between {{shengQiDir}} and the others is a smaller matter.

Two corners, then, are in play for you: the room's North, which you check and keep clear, and the direction {{shengQiDir}}, which you turn the chair toward at the desk. Neither one is a guarantee of anything. The tradition gives the North to path and work and gives Water the job of keeping it flowing; it points the desk toward your generating direction for ambitious work. What you do at the desk is still yours to do. The reading only tells you which way the room is set up to support the doing.

Keep the North clear and moving. Set the desk in command position, facing {{shengQiDir}} when the work is the kind you want to push forward. Those two moves are the whole of the career corner, and both are within your reach this week.
