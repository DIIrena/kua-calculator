You have read the whole book. The four directions that the tradition reads as on your side, and the four it reads as worth treating with care. Pick one move this week. Just one. The bed is the highest-leverage move. The desk is the easiest. The morning corner is the cheapest. Any one of them is enough to test whether the tradition's claim is true *for you*, in *your* home, in *your* week. Seven days is the standard practitioner threshold for noticing whether a small directional shift is doing anything. Pick the move, make it, hold it for the seven days, see what shifts.

Then come back to the book.

## Read it twice

A practitioner once told a student that a real feng shui book is read three times. The first time to follow the argument. The second time to mark the moves you can actually make in your actual home. The third time, months later, to notice which of the moves you made stuck and which ones quietly reverted.

This book is short on purpose. You can read it on a long Saturday morning. Do not be surprised if the second reading lands differently. The compass does not change. You do.

## The thing we will not promise

We will not promise this book changes your life. The tradition does not. The honest claim is smaller and more durable: the structured way of reading your Kua and your eight directions gives you a *vocabulary* for noticing what your nervous system has probably already been telling you, and a *method* for choosing between four equivalent-seeming options. That is what a decision tool is. It is not a fortune.

What we hope you will notice, in the seven days after you move the first thing, is the small shift that comes from your room finally agreeing with you. That shift is not magic. It is what happens when the wind stops being in your face.
